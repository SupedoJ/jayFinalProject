The City

Sweet City Nights

The city has always been my home. Since a young age, I've only ever seen small parts of the outside. I've always wanted to see what was beyond the armed guards and smooth concrete. Despite the adventurer inside of me, the city offers so much that it makes anybody want to stay forever. When the sun settles over the horizon and the moon rises, the city comes to life. The buzzing neon lights, beautiful street dancers, and the many people enjoying themselves can all be seen making the city lively. It's a happy place, but all good things must come to an end.


Hustle & Bustle

The Hustle & Bustle. The beeping of cars, screaming market men, and the screeching of police sirens. Every now and again you might hear gunshots. Where did they come from? Who shot? Wrong questions. Just keep your head down and keep pushing. If you don't bring attention to yourself, then you become a nobody. Being a nobody is about the safest thing you can be. The city's downtown market is where you'll find most of the lights and people. Everyone here is always selling and buying. Doesn't matter what it is, as long as money is tied to it. I like it down here. I only come down here to see Old Man Yuki, though. He sells the best fish. He has a premium fishing license, allowing him access to the outside. When I was a kid he would sometimes sneak me out to see the world. That's where I learned to fish, but that's where I also learned to hide. Old Yuki taught me everything I know about being hidden. How to hold your breath under water for long periods of time, how to fit in small places, and even how to walk and run silently. Back then I thought that's how we caught fish, I couldn't be more wrong. He was preparing me for the cruel reality of our world. "Hey, son! How long has it been?" Yuki asked. "Too long! You still up and kicking!?" I laughed. "Oh you know me. So, how's business?" "Bien." "Oh boy, you know I don't speak that tongue." "Yeah yeah. Still got my favorites, old man?" "Oh yes! Only the best for you." He gave me a plate of two fresh cooked salmon. "Thanks old man, here's a tip." I offered him some of my coin, but he rejected it. "You know I don't need the money," he said. "Suit yourself." As I was walking away he called out to me. "You know the shop will soon be yours! Keep a good head on your shoulders, will ya!?" "Sure thing, Mr. Yuki!" I saw his sweet smile before I disappeared behind the crowds of people, eating my favorite salmon. Yuki has always been there for me ever since he found me in one of his crates. He didn't know who I belonged to, so he took it upon himself to raise me while also trying to manage his business. Though I love seeing him, he's not why I'm downtown. I had a little meet up with one of my friends. I had something for him.


The Alleyways (Danger)

The alleyways is never somewhere you want to be, especially if you don't got any business being there. Too many loose screws there. If you so much as look at one of these guys, you're dead. For me, however, guys like these aren't a problem. My freind told me to meet him in the alley behind Rasputin's. I scoped the area just in case it was set up. I dropped from the ledge above him and started talking business. "Ah! There you are! I've been waiting for hours," he said. His name is Crunch. He's a scrawny fellow, but he's also jittery and quick on his feet. "Stop complaining. I'm here, aren't I?" I replied. "So, you got it?" He asked. "Of course I got it, now where's my payment?" I threw him a level three clearence card. "P-Payment? Haha, y-you see-" I threw him against the wall. "No funny stuff! Pay up, now!" I yelled. "I don't call you Crunch just because," I said, grabbing his arm that was in a cast. "H-Hurt me all you want, but let me tell you this. I-I have two snipers o-on the roof and f-four men here in this alley," he said. I looked around and saw nothing. I raised my fist, ready to punch him. "Not making this easy huh, Crunch?" I swung, but I stopped. I smelled something. Batteries. Not the kind that you would usually smell. It was Eco Batts. "Wraiths!? Are you serious Crunch!?" I dropped him and ran.


The Chase

The only people that use Eco batteries are Wraiths. Wraiths are an elite police force armed with cloaking technology that makes them invisble. The only way to detect them is by the smell of their burning batteries. He sold me out to the feds! Damn it! I knew I couldn't trust him! "Get out my way!" I yelled as I ran through the city, pushing people out my way. I could smell them chasing me. Six chasing me. Two flanking right and two flanking left. Two are jumping from rooftop to rooftop trying to get a good shot. I'm so thankful for this smell of mine. Hopping fences, hurdling over cars, even turning corners is not enough to shake them. I was running until my body went cold. BANG! It was one of the snipers. They had just barely missed my foot. I heard sirens. It was a cruiser flying in front of me. "Stop where you are and put your hands up!" An officer yelled over the speaker. "In your dreams!" I said as I gave them the bird. I hit a hard right and scaled the wall. I was on the roof now. The two Wraiths were now on either side of me. I knew I could get away. Just a little more running. "Ha! Try to keep up! How many times have I gotten awa-" I smacked my face into something I didn't see.I got up from the ground and saw an all white Wraith standing over me. I couldn't smell the Eco Batts off of him. How? "Ma'am, I got him," he said over his earpiece. He shot me with a shock round from his gun. It hurt badly but it was nothing that I wasn't already used to. He picked me up and cuffed me. A fancy police cruiser flew to the building that we were on and a familiar face stepped out. "Hey, Trish. How's about a second date?" I asked playfully. "Maybe in the interrogation room," she responded. "I want him in the car now. Tell the Wraiths on the ground to follow and get those snipers. Tell them to get in the car with us," She commanded. "The Yakuza got you by the neck too?" I asked. She gave no answer. "So who's the big guy?" "His name is I.C.E. An Invisble Clean Ecounit." "That's why I couldn't smell him. When did the police stop polluting the air?" "Since we caught you." "Caught who?" I said with my hands up and out of the cuffs. "What the-" "Bye-bye!" I said as I jumped off the roof. I ran and hid in the shadows. I reached a manhole cover and jumed in the sewers. I knew what my next plan would be. Next Page
